# Decision — Acme Widgets Renewal: 10% Discount Tied to 2-Year Commit

## Decision
Offer Acme Widgets Co a 10% discount on the Platform + Premium support
bundle, conditional on a 2-year commitment, in the renewal proposal.

## Why
- Acme's real risk isn't price sensitivity — it's the unshipped
  bulk-import feature (raised twice, see
  [[04-entities/customers/acme-widgets-co|customer note]]). A longer
  commit buys runway to close that gap before it becomes a churn reason.
- A 2-year term moves them past the next budget cycle, where
  [[04-entities/people/sam-rivera|Sam Rivera]] said procurement
  re-evaluates all vendor contracts.
- 10% is inside the discount band we can approve without escalation;
  anything deeper needs a business case to finance.

## Alternatives considered (and why rejected)
- **Flat 15% discount, 1-year term** — deeper cut with no retention
  benefit; renews the churn conversation in 12 months with the
  bulk-import gap possibly still open.
- **No discount, add bulk-import to the contract as a committed
  deliverable** — product won't commit to a date
  ([[04-entities/teams/product|Product]] has it in discovery, not on
  the roadmap); promising it contractually was ruled out.
- **Usage-based pricing pilot** — interesting but new pricing machinery
  mid-renewal adds risk to a deal we want to be boring.

## Revisit when
Bulk-import ships, or Acme pushes back on the 2-year term in the
proposal review.
